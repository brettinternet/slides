New Process

Prerequisites

  1. Target skill level
  2. Scorecard
  3. Critical competencies
What else do we need before we start?

1. Resume or Assessment

Candidates are selectively filtered based on their work experience.

However, candidates may choose to stand out by completing an assessment.

This step and the next step are performed by full-time recruiting staff.

This is essentially a social call with a fairly predictable choreography.

2. HR phone screening

This is the largest filter.

2. Tech phone screening

Questions from easy to hard, keeping in mind target skill level.

E.g. What's the difference between var, let, and const? When was the last time you had an unanswered question posted to StackOverflow or GitHub?

Tech or guild screening.

3. Primary Interview

  1. Mock pull request
  2. Toy problem or take-home project
  3. Design discussion
  4. Talk shop

What are we missing?

PR: Review a pull request with the candidate. The interviewee has a week prior to the interview to review and test the pull request.

Toy problem vs project: Potentially a pair programming opportunity. Candidate is given the decision prior to the interview. Whiteboarding problems are relevant to the position they're applying for and demonstrate important concepts to the position.

Design: Show an example UX design mockup (front-end) or entity relationship (back-end) and discuss with designers or peer engineers an approach to create an app with specified constraints. Front-end: functional and design questions are discussed; Backend-end: data structures and design patterns are discussed.

Talking shop: Are they asking questions about what everybody does and how things work? Are they curious? Do they treat everyone they meet with respect, and show interest in what they do?

From the NY Times: What kind of animal would you be? And why? What qualities of your parents do you like the most?
